Buyer trigger

You probably don't have an AI adoption problem because people are unwilling to use AI.

You have a control problem if people are already using it differently across teams, managers can't see how outputs are being checked, and leadership has no clear evidence that guidance has reached the point of work.

01

Use is already inside real work.

People use AI in customer, commercial and operational work, but the business has limited visibility.

02

Managers lack a review standard.

Managers don't know how to review AI-assisted outputs consistently or explain what good use looks like.

03

Guidance hasn't changed behaviour.

AI guidance exists, but there is little evidence that people have understood, acknowledged or applied it.

04

Teams create their own habits.

Prompts, shortcuts and review habits vary between people, teams and managers.

05

Productivity claims are hard to evidence.

Useful gains may be real, but leaders can't easily see where value, rework or review burden is showing up.

06

Judgement depends on individual discretion.

Data handling, output review and escalation often depend on the person rather than the operating standard.

How WAIA works

FAQ

Clear boundaries matter.

Is WAIA training or governance?

WAIA is an adoption system. Learning is part of it, but the value is the combination of baseline visibility, shared guidance, manager support and evidence-led follow-up.

Does WAIA monitor employee AI prompts?

No. WAIA is not surveillance. It helps organisations set standards, support people and evidence adoption.

Does WAIA prove compliance?

No. WAIA supports practical adoption evidence, but it is not a compliance certification or regulated assurance product.

Do we need an AI policy first?

No. WAIA can help an organisation start with simple operating guidance, then improve it as adoption matures.

Who should own WAIA internally?

Usually a founder, MD, People leader, Operations leader or senior sponsor responsible for safe and effective AI adoption.

How should a company start?

Start with the AI adoption diagnostic or a WAIA fit call. The first goal is to establish where workplace AI use stands today and where follow-up should focus.
