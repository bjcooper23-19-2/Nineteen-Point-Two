Outside Clarity Scope and process

How the outside view turns into a usable decision document.

Outside Clarity is bounded around one clearly defined decision. The process turns the plan, evidence, assumptions and exposure into a written view of what should happen next.

After submission

What happens next.

01
Submit the decision context

Share the plan, the choice in front of you, the evidence, the assumptions and the constraints.

02
Review the evidence and exposure

The plan is examined for commercial reality, stakeholder response, evidence strength, execution risk and the case against proceeding.

03
Receive the written view

The output sets out the verdict, confidence signal, exposed assumptions and the next move.

Defined decision

One purchase covers one clearly defined decision.

A defined decision might be a new offer, pricing change, AI initiative, website brief, internal business case or operating choice. Larger or multi-part reviews should be scoped separately before work starts.

Good fit

One plan with enough context to understand the audience, desired outcome, constraints, evidence and decision pressure.

Separately scoped

Multiple products, multiple markets, a full operating model, complex legal documents or sensitive customer material.

Levels

Snapshot, Deeper View and Debrief.

Every Outside Clarity review starts with the same structured view of the plan, evidence, assumptions and exposure. What changes is the depth of the written output and whether the result is worked through directly.

Boundaries

Information handling and professional advice.

Information handling

Outside Clarity is designed to work from concise, non-sensitive decision context. Do not submit confidential business information, trade secrets, customer data, employee data, credentials, contracts, financial records, legal documents or regulated information through the intake form.

Important note

Outside Clarity provides structured decision support. It is not legal, financial, investment, tax, employment law or regulated professional advice.
