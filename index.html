Where risk actually enters

Most revenue problems are discovered too late.

Not because teams aren't working hard. Because the system isn't visible early enough. The warning signs usually appear between teams, not inside one team.

01

Forecasts move late.

The number changes after confidence has already been communicated.

02

Retention risk appears at renewal.

But the original failure usually started during handover, onboarding or adoption.

03

More activity does not create more certainty.

Teams get busier while the underlying operating model stays unclear.

04

Leadership debates symptoms.

The real constraint is hidden in ownership, timing, definitions or decision quality.

Core belief

Revenue is a system.

Sales, Customer Success, Operations, Product and Finance all shape the commercial outcome. When their assumptions don't connect, risk compounds quietly.

Signal

What does the business think is true?

Pipeline coverage, adoption health, renewal readiness and capacity assumptions often look credible until they are tested together.

Friction

Where does confidence break down?

Weak handovers, unclear ownership and inconsistent definitions create gaps that reporting can describe but not fix.

Constraint

What is actually limiting performance?

The answer is rarely a single team. It is usually the way people, process and data interact under commercial pressure.

Action

What should change first?

The goal is sharper prioritisation, clearer ownership and better commercial decisions without adding unnecessary operational noise.

What the diagnostic does

It turns a revenue ambition into an operating reality check.

The point is not to produce a neat score. The point is to expose whether the target is commercially believable, where the pressure sits, and what should be fixed first.

01

Model the mechanics.

Test the target against deal size, win rate, sales cycle, rep count, ramp and attainment.

02

Stress the assumptions.

Identify whether the plan relies on fragile assumptions, heroic capacity or unrealistic timing.

03

Read the constraint.

See whether the issue is people, process, data, handover quality, forecast discipline or lifecycle evidence.

Operating model

People, process and data, tested together.

These aren't separate service lines. They are the three places revenue integrity usually breaks down.

PeopleP

Capability, ownership and accountability.

ProcessPr
