What an organisation gets

A lightweight operating layer for organisation-wide AI adoption.

Workplace AI Academy gives an organisation the building blocks for practical rollout: role-aware learner access, organisation-specific AI guidance, guidance acknowledgement, assigned course pathways, completion evidence and admin-level visibility. It works for teams that need a common operating baseline before fragmented use hardens into everyday behaviour.

  • Set organisation guidance that learners can read and acknowledge.
  • Give learners access based on role, cohort or function.
  • Assign governance, applied and manager pathways where needed.
  • Track progress, acknowledgement and completion evidence.
Platform role

From policy intent to day-to-day workplace behaviour.

If you're still clarifying the risk, the readiness problem is described in the Workplace AI overview. The Academy is the mechanism for operationalising shared standards, practical guidance and visible adoption signals.

Learn, apply and govern

Three layers, one consistent adoption baseline.

The Academy combines shared learning, practical application and lightweight governance so AI policy can become consistent workplace behaviour.

Learn01

AI Governance at Work

A foundation course for employees who need shared standards for safe, effective and accountable workplace AI use, including how to recognise the limits of AI-supported work.

Apply02

Applied AI

For teams ready to use AI in defined workflows, with attention to prompt quality, evidence, handover, review and repeatable practice.

Govern03

Managing AI in Work

For managers who need to guide teams, review AI-supported work, spot risk and keep human accountability clear.

Organisation setup

Set guidance, invite learners and assign the right access.

Admins can structure the rollout around the way the business actually works: all employees, specific functions, manager groups or AI-active teams, with guidance that reflects the organisation's own standards.

Set up

Create the organisation baseline.

Add organisation settings, AI guidance and learner groups before invitations are sent, so the rollout starts from shared expectations rather than individual interpretation.

Invite

Send access to the right people.

Learners receive access to the Academy and see the guidance and pathway assigned to them.

Assign

Match access to role and risk.

Use role-aware access and the right pathway depending on what each group needs to do with AI at work and where human review matters most.

Acknowledge

Capture guidance acknowledgement.

Learners acknowledge the organisation's AI guidance so leaders can see whether policy has reached the point of use.

Track

Monitor progress and completion.

Admin views show who has started, who has acknowledged guidance, who has completed and where follow-up is needed.

Learner journey

Each learner sees a clear path through assigned content.

Learners sign in, see their dashboard, read the organisation's AI guidance, acknowledge the standard expected of them and work through the content assigned to their role.

01

Assigned pathway.

The learner sees the course, guidance and pathway selected for their role, team or rollout cohort.

02

AI Guidance area.

The learner has a dedicated place to read organisation guidance and acknowledge the standard before using AI in work.

03

Progress and completion.

Course progress, completion and certificates create a record the organisation can use for rollout evidence and follow-up.

Admin visibility

Admins can see rollout progress rather than guessing.

The admin view is built for operational follow-up. It shows learner status, guidance acknowledgement, pathway coverage, completion evidence and the groups that need reminders or manager support.

01

Learner records.

See who has been invited, which access they have and whether they have started.

02

Guidance acknowledgement.

Check which learners have acknowledged the organisation's AI guidance and which groups still need follow-up.

03

Completion status.

Identify completed, in-progress and outstanding learners, including certificate status where completion applies.

04

Follow-up lists.

Use progress gaps to trigger reminders, manager prompts or targeted support.

05

Evidence trail.

Keep a practical record of assigned learning, guidance acknowledgement, completion and certificate evidence against the rollout plan.

06

Rollout reporting.

Give sponsors a clear read on adoption coverage, not just intent or attendance.

Deployment options

Designed for baseline rollout, targeted pathways or managed adoption programmes.

Organisations can use Workplace AI Academy as a broad foundation, a manager readiness programme, a governance baseline, or a targeted pathway for teams already applying AI in operational work. It does not need a large transformation programme to create useful visibility and consistency.

  • Organisation-wide baseline for safe, consistent and practical use.
  • Role-aware pathways for managers, operators and AI-active teams.
  • Cohort rollout for specific functions or risk areas.
  • Organisation guidance and acknowledgement for policy adoption.
  • Evidence-led support for governance rhythm without heavy enterprise GRC.
